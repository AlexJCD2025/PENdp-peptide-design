================================================================================
PAPER 3: AKPC-modified LNP for p32/CD44-targeted delivery
================================================================================

Full Title: Peptide-Modified Lipid Nanoparticles Boost the Antitumor Efficacy of
RNA Therapeutics
Authors: Zhao et al.
Source: ACS Nano, 2025, 19(14), 13685-13704
DOI: 10.1021/acsnano.4c14625
PMID: 40176316 | PMCID: PMC12004924
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12004924/

Note: The user referenced this as "AKPC-modified LNP for p32-targeted delivery"
(ACS Nano 2024), but the actual paper targets CD44 (not p32) via the A6 peptide
(KPSSPPEE) conjugated to LNPs (AKPC-LNP). The year is 2025, not 2024.

================================================================================
ABSTRACT / KEY INNOVATION
================================================================================

LNPs modified with a CD44-specific lipopeptide (AKPC-LNP) enable targeted delivery
of YAP/TAZ-siRNA (siYT) to CD44+ cancer cells. This is the FIRST example of
CD44-targeted LNPs for RNA interference in metastatic breast cancer.

Background:
- Breast cancer (BC) is a leading cause of cancer death; metastatic BC has 22% 5-year survival
- RNA therapeutics (siRNA) can precisely silence disease-causing genes
- LNPs (gold standard for siRNA delivery, e.g., Onpattro) have limited antitumor
  efficacy due to unspecific hepatic accumulation after systemic administration
- YAP/TAZ: transcriptional co-activators of Hippo pathway; drive proliferation,
  stemness, chemoresistance, metastasis in most solid tumors including breast cancer
- CD44: cancer stem cell marker overexpressed in many cancers

================================================================================
METHODOLOGY HIGHLIGHTS
================================================================================

LIPOPEPTIDE & LNP FORMULATION:

Lipopeptide: Ac-KPSSPPEEGGGKKKGK-PEG4-Chol (AKPC)
  - Synthesized via Fmoc SPPS, purified by HPLC, confirmed by LC-MS
  - The A6 peptide (KPSSPPEE) targets CD44
  - GGGKKKGK spacer improves hydrophilicity and binding affinity
  - Conjugated to cholesterol via PEG4 linker

Control: scrambled peptide (Cho-PEG4-SPEKPEPS-NH2)

LNP Composition (MC3-siYT base):
  - Ionizable lipid Dlin-MC3-DMA: 50 mol%
  - Cholesterol: 38.5 mol%
  - DSPC: 10 mol%
  - DMG-PEG2K: 1.5 mol%
  - For AKPC-LNP: 2 mol% AKPC added

Formulation: Microfluidic mixing at 3:1 flow ratio (aqueous siRNA:lipid ethanol)
→ dialysis against PBS

Physicochemical Properties (unmodified vs. AKPC):
  - Hydrodynamic diameter: ~120 nm (PDI < 0.2)
  - Zeta potential: near-neutral
  - Encapsulation efficiency: >90%
  - Cryo-TEM: similar morphology (electron-dense core, some lamellar structures)

CELLS & MODELS:
  - In vitro: Human triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC) lines HCC38 and MDA-MB-231
    (high CD44 expression)
  - In vivo: Zebrafish xenograft model (Tg(fli1:EGFP)/Casper)
  - Additional: Prostate cancer PDX-derived organoids (LAPC9) and subcutaneous PDX

================================================================================
KEY RESULTS
================================================================================

1. CD44-DEPENDENT TARGETING IN VITRO
- Flow cytometry & confocal microscopy showed significantly higher binding/uptake
  of AKPC-siYT vs. unmodified MC3-siYT in both HCC38 and MDA-MB-231 cells
- CD44 knockdown by lentiviral shRNA abolished enhanced binding → confirms specificity
- Scrambled peptide-LNPs did not increase uptake

2. IN VIVO TUMOR TARGETING (ZEBRAFISH)
- MC3-siYT: no colocalization with tumor cells (Pearson correlation ~0.0)
- AKPC-siYT: strong colocalization (PCC > 0.5) in both tail and hindbrain
- Scrambled controls: no targeting in vivo

3. IN VITRO ANTITUMOR EFFICACY (2D)
- Gene silencing (qPCR, 48h): AKPC-siYT silenced YAP/TAZ mRNA by 85% (HCC38),
  75% (MDA-MB-231) vs. MC3-siYT ~50%
- Downstream genes (AMOTL2, CTGF, CYR61) also suppressed more strongly
- Apoptosis (Annexin V/PI): HCC38: PBS 9.6%, MC3-siYT 14.3%, AKPC-siYT 40.1%
- Cell viability (WST-1, 72h): AKPC-siYT reduced to 28% (HCC38), 33% (MDA-MB-231)
- Colony formation: AKPC-siYT almost completely abolished; MC3-siYT only partially

4. 3D TUMOR SPHEROID MODEL
- AKPC-siYT induced strongest apoptosis (Flip:EGFP/mCherry ratio)
- Spheroid volume: AKPC-siYT reduced by ~70% (vs. MC3-siYT ~40%, controls ~0%)

5. IN VIVO ANTITUMOR EFFICACY (ZEBRAFISH)
- In tail (systemic): AKPC-siYT induced ~100% tumor suppression
- In hindbrain: AKPC-siYT produced strongest inhibition
