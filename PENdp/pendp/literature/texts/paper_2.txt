CENDR MECHANISM — COMPREHENSIVE REVIEW (Teesalu, Sugahara, Ruoslahti 2013)
================================================================================

ABSTRACT (Key Excerpts):

"Tumor-homing peptides can be used to deliver drugs into tumors. ... The prototypic
peptide of this class, iRGD (CRGDKGPDC), contains the integrin-binding RGD motif.
... The tumor-penetrating properties of iRGD are mediated by a second sequence motif,
R/KXXR/K. This C-end Rule (or CendR) motif is active only when the second basic
residue is exposed at the C-terminus of the peptide."

"Proteolytic processing of iRGD in tumors activates the cryptic CendR motif, which
then binds to neuropilin-1 activating an endocytic bulk transport pathway through
tumor tissue."

"Remarkably the drug to be targeted does not have to be coupled to the peptide; the
bulk transport system activated by the peptide sweeps along any compound that is
present in the blood."

================================================================================
1. BACKGROUND — THE DRUG PENETRATION PROBLEM
================================================================================

- Systemic drug delivery: Only a small fraction reaches the target.
- Synaphic (active) targeting uses molecular markers specific to target tissue.
- Limitations: Even when delivered to tumor vessels, drugs must still extravasate
  and penetrate into extravascular tumor tissue to reach cancer cells.
- Receptor number limit: A gram of tumor tissue likely has only a few picomoles of
  any given receptor — insufficient for effective conjugated delivery.

================================================================================
2. TUMOR-PENETRATING PEPTIDES — CORE MECHANISM
================================================================================

MODULAR STRUCTURE:

Tumor-penetrating peptides (e.g., iRGD, LyP-1, iNGR) contain three modules:
1. Vascular homing motif — binds primary receptor on tumor endothelium (RGD → αv integrins)
2. R/KXXR/K tissue penetration motif (CendR motif) — becomes active after cleavage
3. Protease recognition site — between the two motifs, cleaved by tumor proteases

PROTOTYPE: iRGD (CRGDKGPDC)

iRGD MECHANISM — 3-STEP TUMOR TARGETING:

| Step | Event                              | Binding Partner           |
|------|-------------------------------------|---------------------------|
| 1    | Vascular homing via RGD             | αv integrins (tumor ECs)  |
| 2    | Proteolytic processing exposes      | Tumor microenvironment    |
|      | C-terminal CendR motif              |                           |
| 3    | CendR binding to neuropilin-1       | NRP-1 (transmembrane)     |
| 4    | Activation of bulk transport        | Endocytic trans-tissue    |
|      | pathway (CendR pathway)             | transport                 |

THE C-END RULE (CENDR) MOTIF:

- Consensus: R/KXXR/K (REQUIRES free C-terminal basic residue)
- Activity lost if: additional amino acids added, C-terminus amidated, D-amino acids
- Saturable, chiral recognition → specific receptor (not like CPPs)
- Identified NRP-1 as main binding molecule via affinity chromatography with RPARPAR

NEUROPILIN-1 (NRP-1):

- Co-receptor for VEGFs, semaphorins, TGFβ — all have C-terminal CendR motifs
- Overexpressed in tumors (cancer cells, stromal cells) and tumor vessels
- Binding pocket: b1b2 domain
- Stable binding requires interaction of -2 and -3 residues with loop III of NRP-1 b1
- D-conformer of RPARPAR does not fit → D-Tat does not bind NRP-1

Physiological ligands with CendR: VEGF, semaphorins, TGFβ, complement C3a/C5a,
bradykinin, kallidin. Pathogens: HTLV-2, Crimean-Congo HFV, tick-borne encephalitis,
Ebola, anthrax toxin (furin cleavage creates active CendR).

THE CENDR TRANSPORT PATHWAY:

- Active, energy-dependent (not diffusion)
- Endosomal uptake into intracellular vesicles
- Extravasation & tumor penetration via endosome-plasma membrane fusion (exocytosis)
- Pathway may be hijacked by viruses and toxins for cell entry and tissue spreading

================================================================================
3. KEY IN VIVO DATA
================================================================================

iRGD Characteristics:
- Affinity for αvβ3: Kd = 17.8 ± 8.6 nM
- Affinity for αvβ5: Kd = ~62 nM
- CRGDK fragment affinity for NRP-1: Kd ~1.4 µM

Drug Penetration (Sugahara et al., Science 2010):
- Co-administration of iRGD increases drug penetration up to 40-fold
- Works for compounds of different sizes and chemical properties
- No chemical conjugation required (co-administration is sufficient)
- Active transport via NRP-1; blocked by NRP-1 antibodies, sodium azide, reduced temp

Tumor Models: Prostate, breast, pancreas, brain, bone, de novo transgenic models
(pancreatic and cervical cancer). Normal tissues were negative.

Nanoparticle Delivery: iRGD-coated phage (65 nm), micelles (15-25 nm), iron oxide
nanoworms (~80 nm) all penetrated deep into tumor tissue.

iRGD-Abraxane (130 nm albumin-paclitaxel nanoparticles):
- 8-fold more accumulation vs. non-targeted abraxane in orthotopic 22Rv1 model
- 11-fold more in BT474 breast cancer model
- Significant tumor growth inhibition at 3 mg/kg (where untargeted had no effect)

================================================================================
4. NATURE REVIEWS CANCER 2010 HIGHLIGHT
================================================================================

"Drugs hitch a ride" — Nature Reviews Cancer, volume 10, page 385 (2010)
Author: Mhairi Skinner (Consulting Editor)

Key points about Ruoslahti's iRGD work:
- iRGD cyclic peptide (CRGDKGPDC) targets αv integrins on tumor endothelium
- After binding, iRGD is cleaved and shows increased binding to NRP-1
- Co-administration of iRGD increases drug penetration up to 40-fold
- Requires active transport, not passive leakage
- Provides a "hitchhiker" mechanism for anticancer drugs
