================================================================================
ORIGINAL ASSET PLATFORM (Veiga et al., Nature Communications 2018)
================================================================================

TITLE: Cell specific delivery of modified mRNA expressing therapeutic proteins
to leukocytes

AUTHORS: Núria Veiga, Meir Goldsmith, Yahel Granot, Dina Rosenblum, Noa Dammes,
Rivka Kedmi, Srinivas Ramishetti, Dan Peer (Tel Aviv University)

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

"Here, we present a precision protein expression strategy in Ly6c+ inflammatory
leukocytes in inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) induced mice. We demonstrate a
therapeutic effect in an IBD model by targeted expression of the interleukin 10
in Ly6c+ inflammatory leukocytes."

Key achievements:
- First in vivo targeted delivery system for selective mmRNA-based protein
  expression in leukocytes
- Used ASSET (Anchored Secondary scFv Enabling Targeting) platform to coat LNPs
  with anti-Ly6c antibodies
- Achieved ~100-fold increase in reporter expression in Ly6c+ cells vs. control
- Therapeutic IL-10 expression reduced colitis pathology in DSS mouse model

================================================================================
METHODOLOGY
================================================================================

ASSET PLATFORM:
- Engineered anti-rat IgG2a single-chain variable fragment (scFv) with N-terminal
  lipid modification
- Anchored into LNP surface via hydrophobic interaction of the lipid moiety
- Captures unmodified antibodies by binding their Fc domain
- Allows rapid switching between different targeting antibodies (modular)

LNP CHARACTERIZATION:
- Size: 63.7 nm ± 1.59 nm (DLS)
- Encapsulation efficiency: ~100% (RiboGreen assay)
- Composition: DLin-MC3-DMA / DSPC / cholesterol / DMG-PEG / DSPE-PEG
  (50:10.5:38:1.4:0.1 mol ratio)
- Fabrication: NanoAssemblr microfluidic mixing

mmRNA SYNTHESIS:
- CleanCap Firefly luciferase and custom IL-10 mmRNA (TriLink)
- Modified nucleotides (5mC, pseudo-uridine) for reduced immunogenicity

================================================================================
KEY RESULTS
================================================================================

IN VIVO TARGETING:
- ~20-fold increase in fLuc expression in intestine (targeted vs. isotype control)
- ~10-fold lower fLuc expression in liver (improved off-target)
- ~100-fold increase in fLuc in Ly6c+ sorted splenocytes
- No difference in Ly6c- population → cell-specific targeting

THERAPEUTIC EFFICACY IN DSS COLITIS MODEL:
- IV injections on days 3, 6, 9 (1 mg/kg mmRNA)
- Significant increase in colonic IL-10 levels
- Significant reduction in TNFα and IL-6 in colon
- Prevention of weight loss, colon shortening, histological damage
- Therapeutic effect lasted >48h (vs. few hours for recombinant IL-10)

SAFETY PROFILE:
- Liver enzymes (ALT, AST, ALP) in normal range
- Normal liver histology (no necrosis, bleeding)
- Normal complete blood count
- No increase in splenic TNFα or IL-6

================================================================================
COMPARISON WITH NEWER TP1107 SYSTEM (Nature Nanotechnology 2025)
================================================================================

A newer antibody-capture system was published in Nature Nanotechnology 2025
(Chen MZ, Yuen D, McLeod VM, et al., DOI: 10.1038/s41565-025-01954-9).

Title: "A versatile antibody capture system drives specific in vivo delivery of
mRNA-loaded lipid nanoparticles"

Key improvements over ASSET:
- Uses optimally oriented anti-Fc nanobody (TP1107) with site-specific incorporation
  of p-azido-phenylalanine at Gln15
- >1,000-fold higher protein expression vs. non-targeted LNPs
- >8-fold higher expression vs. conventional random antibody conjugation
- ~50% of CD4+ and CD4+ T cells targeted in spleen and liver in vivo
- Minimal off-target delivery to other immune cells
- No significant toxicity observed

Orientation Strategy Comparison:
| Method                     | Orientation             | Complexity                   |
|----------------------------|-------------------------|------------------------------|
| Random lysine (NHS)        | Random (± variable)     | Simple but impairs binding   |
| Reduced hinge (maleimide)  | Fixed but not optimal   | Requires careful conditions  |
| ASSET (lipidated scFv)     | Suboptimal (N-terminal) | Simple but limited           |
| Optimal TP1107 (azPhe-Q15) | Optimal                 | Simple, high yield           |
